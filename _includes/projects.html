Full-body Sensing Network Based Motor Skill Coaching System

This research aims to develop a novel coaching system that can substantially improve motor learning efficiency for certain sports skills. We developed an 18-channel 6-axis full-body wearable sensor network in order to obtain real-time, precise biomechanical data for high-performance movement analysis. Golf putting stroke is selected as our target skill in the experiment. We are then able to compare movement patterns of professional athletes and amateur players and derive necessary on-line feedback signals to help the amateur players' movement converge to the professionals'. In the end, we also attempted to develop a self-evaluation sub-system using event-related potential (ERP) so we know if the user reacted to our feedback timely or not.

Timing Issue in Power-assisted System

This is a continuation of the previous work on the exoskeleton. We investigated the commonly neglected timing issue in power-assisted devices and designed an EMG based evaluation method to verify our hypothesis. As a result, we achieved above-the-norm performance with well-timed assistance than most of the other proportional-EMG-controlled human augmentation devices. A journal paper has been published in SICE Journal of Control, Measurement, and System Integration titled On the Well-Timed Assistance in Power-Assisted Sit-to-Stand Movement.

Lower-Body Exoskeleton

We designed a lower-body exoskeleton for power assistance in daily activities. Our prototype has been through several iterations. The initial concept was to incorporate energy recycling mechanism so the suit could be quasi-passive and energy efficient. We then adopted the tensegrity structure in Architecture in order to reduce the overall size. The final exoskeleton is more similar to traditional designs, which employed one air cylinder each leg as the main actuators. Encoders are also mounted on hip, knee and ankle joints for gait detection and movement analysis.

Renesas Super MCU Car Rally (China)

A smart line-tracking car built on top of the Renesas H8/3048F-ONE microcontroller. The goal is to race on an unknown track at maximum speed with only eight IR sensors in front. We customized the car using high-performance coreless DC motors and encoder and achieved ultra low failure rate at 3.2m/s. I worked as the algorithm architect with my team and won the 1st prize winner.
